      Excerpt from book "The Surnames of Scotland- Their Origin,
      Meaning and History" by George Frasier Black. 1946

KEDDIE: Kedie, Keddy, Kiddie, Kiddy. The ship of John Kid, a
Scotsman, was wrecked at Holkham, Norfolk, England, in 1388.
John Kady in record at Dysart, 1577, Margaret Keddie in Falsyde,
ParishofRoberton, 1623, Dorathia Kadie, heir of Alexander tailor
in Edinburgh, 1616, and James Keddie, hat maker there in 1635.
David Kediein Weyms, 18630, James Keddie was bewitched there in
1639, and Robert Keddie was elder in Dysart in 1641. Alexander Ceddy
was fined for 'Straicks and ryot' in 1664 and Donald Keddie in Moy,
Caithness, to be apprehended as a rebel in 1670. From (Mac) Keddie.
The name John Kede was probably from another source.
MacKEDDIE, MacKiddie: Forms of MacAdie. Donald M'Keddie, piper
in King Edward, was accused in 1647 'for drinking the devil's
health and pyping to those who drank it.' The form Maceddie is
found in Inverness. McKeddey 1684 and MacKedy 1647.
